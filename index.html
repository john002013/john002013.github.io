01 / About

From Scrap Robots to
Cutting-Edge Research

My journey in robotics began at age 8, building robots from scraps. Today, I'm preparing to shape the next generation of roboticists.

I am a final year student from the Department of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, Ladoke Akintola University of Technology, currently standing as the 2nd best graduating student of my department for the class of 2026.

During the course of my undergraduate journey, and having developed a passion for robotics starting from the age of 8 where I built robots using scraps, I immersed myself in many self-paced learning curves. I learned ROS (Robot Operating System), Embedded Systems, Computer Vision, PyTorch advanced deep learning techniques, technical report documentation, and more.

My long-term goal is to become a professor of robotics, holding the belief that the best way to impact the world in a positive way is to teach robotics. It is interesting, and any child exposed to it at a young age will stay glued to it. I am committed to exposing, teaching, and giving back to my society in terms of STEM education, innovation, and research.
